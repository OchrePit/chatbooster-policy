This policy explains what cookies and similar technologies are used on ChatBooster's marketing website and within the ChatBooster account application, what they are used for, and how you can control them.

1. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by a website you visit. They allow the site to recognize your device, remember preferences, keep you signed in, and measure how the site is used.

2. Categories of cookies we use

  • Strictly necessary cookies. Required to operate the site and the signed-in application — for example, to keep you logged in, remember your selected audience view (agency or creator), and protect against unauthorized session activity. These cookies cannot be switched off through the site.
  • Functional cookies. Remember choices you make so the site behaves the way you expect — for example, preferred language, recently viewed pricing pack, or audience selection on the landing page.
  • Analytics cookies. Help us understand how visitors arrive at and move through the site so we can improve it. We use aggregated, non-identifying data for this purpose.

We do not use cookies to build advertising profiles of individuals, and we do not sell personal information.

3. Third-party cookies

Some pages may embed content from third parties — for example, video players (Loom) and scheduling widgets (Calendly). Those third parties may set their own cookies when their content is loaded. We do not control those cookies. Their use is governed by the third party's own privacy and cookie policies.

4. How long cookies stay

Session cookies are deleted when you close your browser. Persistent cookies remain on your device until they expire or you delete them. The expiration period varies by cookie purpose, generally between 30 days and 13 months.

5. How you can control cookies

You can accept or decline non-essential cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers also let you delete cookies that have already been set. Removing strictly necessary cookies may affect functionality — for example, you may be signed out automatically and required to log in again.

For consent-based control across visits, you can also use your browser's privacy or "Do Not Track" settings. We honor browser-level privacy signals where they are technically supported.

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. Material updates will be reflected on this page with a new "Last updated" date.
